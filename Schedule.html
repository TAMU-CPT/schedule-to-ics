Explanation of class schedule:

        The course consists of three types of sessions:  L, for in-class lecture/exam periods; C, computer laboratory periods; and B, bench laboratory periods.  L and C sessions are held in BioBio 106; B sessions are held in BioBio 243 and in BioBio 312, the CPT laboratory.  The schedule may be changed at any time; changes will be reflected in the Google Calendar and in this document..

        The course is front-loaded with L sessions.  The reason for this is that it is necessary to equip you with the background knowledge and terminology of bacteriophage biology before diving into the genomic analysis.  Thus your first computer laboratory is not until Feb. 11. Note that L sessions covered by the two exams terminate in mid April.  After that time, classroom activities are mainly devoted to working on the genome analyses, although “mini-lectures” may be presented within the C sessions to take advantage of discoveries made by the students.

        All of the scheduled bench laboratory activities will be on Fridays.  Since this is a real research-based course and you are dealing with microbiology, there is no way to confine all the necessary activities to a rigid time slot.  There will be times when you must come in at an odd hour, perhaps even on the weekend, to gather a sample, terminate a gel, harvest a culture, etc.  In addition, it may be necessary for you to do some travel about the Brazos Valley, at minimum, to harvest environmental samples.

        We estimate that the 15 L sessions,13 C sessions and 14 B sessions will add up to approximately 3 semester credit hours of required attendance.  Of course, you are certainly welcome to devote more time to the course.  Sometimes students get very attached to "their own" bacteriophages and just cannot tear themselves away!
